It indicates that Energy Consumption may have a positive correlation to the Happiness according to the GDP and HDI relationship​. It is because the GDP and HDI are the main factors that affect the happiness of the country. Generally, higher GDP leads to better public services and job opportunities, while a higher HDI indicates improved education and health, both contributes to a better quality of life.
And the energy consumption is the main factor that affects the GDP and HDI. Because it drives industrial production, supports economic activity, and ensures access to essential services like education and healthcare, all contributs to economic growth and improved living standards. Higher energy consumption typically indicates greater economic output and the ability to invest in infrastructure and social programs.

To conclude, it shows that the higher energy consumption may lead to a higher GDP and HDI, which may lead to a higher happiness score.

* GDP (Gross Domestic Product): The total monetary value of all final goods and services produced in a country within a specific time period, usually measured annually. It indicates the economic performance of a country.
* CPI (Consumer Price Index): A measure that examines the weighted average of prices of a basket of consumer goods and services, such as food, transportation, and medical care. It is used to estimate inflation and the cost of living over time.
* HDI (Human Development Index): A composite index that measures a country's average achievements in three basic aspects of human development: health (life expectancy), education (years of schooling), and standard of living (GNI per capita). It aims to assess overall human well-being rather than purely economic factors.

Conclusion

The above Link-Node Graph shows that:
1. Increased energy consumption can drive economic growth, which may enhance overall happiness.
2. Higher levels of energy consumption are associated with improved human development, leading to greater happiness among citizens.
3. The Consumer Price Index (CPI) reflects inflation; rising prices of goods can negatively impact happiness.
4. Air pollution significantly affects individual well-being and happiness.

Overall Insight:
In summary, greater energy consumption contributes to economic growth and improved living standards, thereby increasing individual happiness. However, it is essential to manage air pollutants generated during energy production to minimize their adverse effects on overall well-being.
